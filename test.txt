CPU Scheduling Simulator Test Cases

Input format reminders:
FCFS/SJF: N, Arrivals, Bursts
Priority: N, Arrivals, Bursts, Priorities (higher number = higher priority)
RR: N, Arrivals, Bursts, Quantum

FCFS Tests :-

Test 1: Basic run, sequential arrival
Input:
4
0 1 2 3
5 3 8 6
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 11.25, Avg WT ~ 5.75

Test 2: Same arrival times, check PID tie-break
Input:
4
0 0 2 2
5 3 4 2
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 8.75, Avg WT ~ 5.25

Test 3: Test with idle CPU time
Input:
4
0 5 6 12
3 6 4 2
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 5.75, Avg WT ~ 2.0

SJF (Non-Preemptive) Test :-

Test 1: Shorter job arrives later
Input:
4
0 1 2 4
5 3 6 4
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 9.0, Avg WT ~ 4.5

SJF (Preemptive) Test :-

Test 1: Testing preemption (P1 should interrupt P0)
Input:
4
0 1 2 3
8 4 9 5
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 13.0, Avg WT ~ 6.5

Priority (Non-Preemptive) Test :-

Test 1: Higher priority runs first, FCFS tie-break
Input:
4
0 1 2 3
10 1 2 1
3 4 5 2
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 9.25, Avg WT ~ 5.75

Priority (Preemptive) Test :-

Test 1: Testing priority preemption (P2 interrupts P0, P3 interrupts P2)
Input:
4
0 1 2 4
5 4 2 1
2 1 3 4
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 5.5, Avg WT ~ 2.5

Round Robin Test :-

Test 1: Basic RR, quantum = 4
Input:
4
0 1 2 3
10 5 8 4
4
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 20.25, Avg WT ~ 13.5

Test 2: Smaller quantum = 2, more switches
Input:
5
0 1 2 3 4
5 3 1 2 3
2
Expected: Avg TAT ~ 8.6, Avg WT ~ 5.8
